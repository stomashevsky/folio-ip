The Red Flags Rule requires businesses to create a plan to identify and prevent identity theft. Find out who this rule applies to and how to comply.

Key takeaways

The Red Flags Rule is an FTC rule that requires certain businesses and organizations to develop and implement a written program designed to detect, prevent, and mitigate identity theft.

Any financial institution or creditor that has “covered accounts” (customer accounts used for personal, family, or household purposes and allows or involves multiple transactions) must follow the Red Flags Rule.

The 4 steps to complying with the Red Flags Rule are identifying red flags, detecting red flags, preventing and mitigating identity theft, and continuously updating your program.
