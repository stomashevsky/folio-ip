While it may be possible to completely automate your business’s fraud detection processes, it shouldn’t necessarily be your goal to do so. Learn why.

Key takeaways

While automation offers a number of benefits such as greater speed and lower labor costs, _full_ automation can bring unintended consequences

Completely automating your fraud detection efforts makes you vulnerable to spoofing techniques that are specifically designed to fool automated systems, such as and face masks. Additionally, you may see an increase in false negatives, especially when that automation is paired with strict or rigid logic and rules.

Adding manual review can enhance your automations by increasing edge case conversion and reducing unnecessary auto-declines, increasing accuracy rates and catching more fraud, helping you identify emerging fraud patterns more quickly, and more.
