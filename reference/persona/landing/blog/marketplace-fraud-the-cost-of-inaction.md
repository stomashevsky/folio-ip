Key takeaways

Failing to address marketplace fraud can result in damaged customer trust, lower revenue, and regulatory risks.

Fake real estate listings, counterfeit goods, spoofed transactions, account takeover scams, promotion schemes, and chargeback fraud are just a few examples of marketplace fraud.

Marketplaces can reduce the risk of fraud by establishing a trust and safety team, performing identity verification during onboarding, conducting ongoing monitoring and link analysis, and more.
