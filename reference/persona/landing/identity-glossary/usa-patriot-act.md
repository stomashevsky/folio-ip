# USA PATRIOT Act: Definition and FAQs

The **USA PATRIOT Act** is a U.S. law designed to deter terrorism passed in the wake of the September 11, 2001 terrorist attacks. It contains provisions related to money laundering and the financing of terrorism. Title III of the law is specifically known as the International Money Laundering Abatement and Financial Anti-Terrorism Act of 2001, which expands on the [Bank Secrecy Act (BSA)](./bank-secrecy-act-bsa.md) and related AML laws.
